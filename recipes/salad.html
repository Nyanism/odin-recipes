Sashimi Salad

Description

What better way to start things off than with a refreshing bowl of sashimi salad? This simple recipe for tossing together a delectable treat is sure to bring even the mermaids for a taste test. Fresh greens, combined with a tangy sauce, will open your appitite for the other food listed on this website.

The end result of this dish is highly dependent on the quality of sashimi used, and as such, I would recommend that you talk to your local sushi bar or fishmonger to work something out. If done right, this recipe would be able to serve about 4 people or so.

Ingredients

  • 4 oranges
  • 2 limes
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons grapeseed oil
  • 6 cups mache lettuce leaves
  • 2 cups frisee lettuce leaves
  • 1 cucumber, peeled, seeded and thinly sliced
  • 200g sushi-grade sashimi of your liking
  • 1 teaspoon seasame seeds, toasted

Steps

  1. Grate the zest of 1 orange and the lime into a large bowl. Squeeze 1 tablespoon of juice from the grated orange and 2 tablespoons of juice from the grated lime into the bowl. Whisk in the soy sauce and the oil. Set the dressing aside.
  2. Use a sharp knife to slice the rind and pith from the remaining oranges. Working over a mixing bowl, cut between the membranes to free the orange segments, letting them drip into the bowl. Pour any juice into a cup and reserve it for another use. Add the mache, frisee and cucumbers to the orange segments.
  3. Using a large, sharp knife, cut the sashimi into 1/3-inch-thick slices. Add the sashimi to the bowl. Drizzle with the dressing, and toss gently to coat. Allow the flavours to meld for at least 2 minutes.
  4. Divide the salad among 4 serving plates. Sprinkle with the toasted sesame seeds, and serve.
